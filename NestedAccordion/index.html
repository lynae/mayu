    • Comp Sci Degree

      One of the biggest problems with getting a formal education for web design and development is that the industry changes too quickly. New technology and new trends mean that by the time you are done with your education much of what you have learned will already be obsolete or at least somewhat dated.

      Quote

      "It's nice you have a Comp Sci degree... it's nice that you are Oracle Certified... but based on your lack of experience, I cannot say you are the right fit for this position." -interviewer, The Developer's Life Education Podcast

      Numbers

      1 in 11 recent CS graduates is unemployed

    • Boot Camps

      In these programs you work in pair programming, on several exercises, and have can sit through local guest lectures-- but what about the staff running your organization? How many years experience does your instructor have? What projects or companies have they contributed to?

      Quote

      "Programming boot camps can't serve as a substitute for real-world experience," says Jon LeBlanc, head of developer evangelism at PayPal

      Numbers

      $10,000 - $20,000 average upfront tuition

    • Self Taught

      Taking this route has major financial advantages compared to spending tens of thousands of dollars on a formal education or bootcamp program. You can also specify your curriculum and learn exactly what you want to learn. On the downside, without a mentor you are likely to learn out-of-date technologies or methods. This often results in educational gaps and obvious lack of understanding coding best practices.

      Quote

      "Being self-taught has been a real hindrance in finding a job," - David S. Gallant, self-taught web developer

      Numbers

      The majority of developers are self taught.
